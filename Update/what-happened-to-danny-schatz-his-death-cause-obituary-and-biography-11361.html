After a prolonged fight with pancreatic cancer, Danny Schatz, 74, passed away on June 26 at the Hospice of Red River Valley.

A race car driver in the 1970s, Danny passed down his passion for racing to his son Donny Schatz. The itch to race for young Donny began when his father would take him to the Knoxville Nationals or the World of Outlaws performance in Fargo.

It was Donny’s turn to take the reins of a sprint car in 1993. The hood and top wing of the vehicle were painted gold with 15, his father’s number, and Schatz Crossroads was prominently emblazoned on both.

Race Car Driver: Danny Schatz Obituary

Danny Schatz, age 74, passed away on June 26 at the Hospice of Red River Valley in North Dakota.

In the 1970s, Danny Schatz competed in local Sprint Car races in North Dakota, Minnesota, Wisconsin, and Ontario. But he made the decision to put his helmet away in the late 1970s.

By the age of 25, Danny Schatz had a stable career working as an electrician for a railroad business in North Dakota, but he desired more.

Together with his wife Diane, they took the risky but successful step of opening their own truck stop, Schatz Crossroads Truck Stop in Minot, North Dakota.

Danny Schatz Cause Of Death Disclosed

The iconic American race car racer Danny Schatz died as a result of pancreatic cancer, which he had been battling since 2019. His funeral arrangements are now being made by his family, and a date for the memorial service has not yet been determined.

Over the course of more than three decades, he was a regular at World of Outlaws events, leaving behind a distinctive legacy that was full of sharp humor.

After working as a railroad employee and subsequently as an ironworker, Danny gradually understood the importance of having a business to succeed in life. He was able to start a truck stop business, Schatz Crossroads, thanks to a friend and sponsor.

Danny entered the corporate world more as a result of his involvement in racing than the other way around.

Insight On American Race Car Driver Danny Schatz Family

Late in the evening, after Danny Schatz passed away, his family issued a statement confirming the tragedy on Twitter. The Schatz family—wife Diane, son Donny, and daughter Deanne—all are mourning the loss of their patriarch.

Danny Schatz bought his son, Donny Schatz, a go-kart when he was 11 years old. In addition to developing a prosperous business, Danny supported Donny throughout his Sprint Car career.

Regarding his father’s support, Schatz remarked in a 2019 interview that there was no way he could have done this or reached this level without his father.

In due course, Danny and Diane Schatz acquired Donny Schatz Racing and became the car’s owners. Prior to joining Tony Stewart Racing, Donny Schatz raced his parents’ car and won two World of Outlaws championships in it.
