Maisie Williams Upbringing And Parents

Maisie Williams was born her parents, father Gary Williams and mother Hilary Pitt Frances on April 15, 1997, in Bristol, England. She was born Margaret Constance Williams.

Maisie’s parents split up when she was just four months old.

She was raised in the Somerset village of Clutton by her mother, who quit her work as a course administrator at a university to pursue Maisie’s acting career.

Since she was a small child, Margaret has gone by the moniker “Maisie,” which originated with her favorite cartoon character from the British comic strip The Perishers. Maisie attended Norton Hill School and Clutton Primary School for her education.

However, her upbringing was not always sunshine and rainbows. She recently opened up about her upbringing with her dad and explained that she had a traumatic relationship with her biological dad which consumed most of her childhood.

During a podcast with Stephen Bartlett on the Diary of A CEO podcast, Maise talked about her traumatic experience with her dad.

She explained that her mother had to escape from her dad when she was just four months old. Her parents divorced and Maise always had a traumatic experience with her biological dad growing up.

Due to her shaky relationship with her dad, she always had trouble sleeping at night. She explained that the things happening to her back then, she did not realize were so impactful that it is still haunting her today. She was too young to differentiate between what is right and wrong and looking back at it now, she shudders at the thought.

However, she is very close to her mother and her stepfather. She was raised by her mother for most of her life, and she adores her mother. Even with a traumatic childhood, Maisie feels like her acting career has helped her make amends for most of her past experiences.

Maisie Williams Upbringing And Childhood

Young Maisie experienced an unremarkable childhood in Clutton, according to her. She was raised in a public housing development in Britain, went to regular schools, and earned regular grades. Maisie grew up with her older siblings at Clutton in Somerset, England, where her mother reared her on her own.

Despite this, Maisie clung to her aspirations of being a successful dancer because she thought they would have a significant impact on her life. Before continuing on to Norton Hill School in Midsomer Norton, Maisie had her foundational education at Clutton Primary School.

Maisie attended high school and took dancing classes at Susan Hill’s School of Dance in addition to her coursework. When Maisie was 11 years old, her dance instructor persuaded her to register for a talent show that featured workshops and seminars designed to assist kids to develop their acting, dancing, and singing talents.

Her Early Career

After participating in the talent event, Maisie met Louise Johnson, an agent, who encouraged her to try out for acting opportunities. Maisie wasn’t one to easily give up on her ambitions to pursue a career as a dancer and establish herself as a professional. Nevertheless, she rose to the occasion and continued on to a Nanny McPhee and the Big Bang audition.

A determined Maisie gave it to her all throughout the auditions and made it to the final two, but she was unable to land any roles in either of the television programs because she just appeared too young. Moving on, Maisie Williams went on an acting trial and landed the role of Arya Stark, a tomboyish avenging swordswoman on the popular HBO television series “Game of Thrones.”

She got favorable reviews for her performance in the series, which debuted in 2011, and was honored in 2012 with the Portal Award for Best Young Actor as well as the Portal Award for Best Supporting Actress in Television. The rest is merely history.

Maisie Williams Career After Game Of Thrones

The Game of Thrones filming schedule gave Maisie Williams the freedom to pursue further acting possibilities. On the BBC miniseries The Secret of Crickley Hall, which was based on renowned British horror author James Herbert’s book of the same name, she played one of her first parts outside of Game of Thrones.

The next films she appeared in were Heatstroke, Gold, The Falling, and The Book of Love. Williams also had an appearance in the Doctor Who TV show. She also appeared in iBoy, Mary Shelley, and Then Came You as her budding career progressed. Her most problematic production then followed.

Daisy Chain Productions, a tiny business that started in early 2016 and makes short films, is also co-founded by Maisie Williams. Given her rocky past with social media, Williams also contributed to the creation of the social networking app Daisie.

Because of her popularity in Game of Thrones, Maisie Williams now has a lot of freedom to explore the world of filmmaking. Williams has recently made appearances on the horror film The Owners as well as the British TV series Two Weeks to Live.
