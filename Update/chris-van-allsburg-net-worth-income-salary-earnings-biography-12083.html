Chris Van Allsburg (born June 18, 1949) is an American artist and author of children’s books who lives in Los Angeles, California. Jumanji (1981) and The Polar Express (1985), both of which he also authored, were both nominated for Caldecott Medals for best picture book artwork in the United States. Both books were subsequently turned into successful movie films. In 1980, with The Garden of Abdul Gasazi, he was a runner-up for the Caldecott Medal. He was nominated for the biannual, worldwide Hans Christian Andersen Award in 1986 for his work as a children’s book illustrator. The Hans Christian Andersen Award is the highest international honour for creators of children’s books. During his time at the University of Michigan, he was awarded an honorary Doctor of Humane Letters degree in April of 2012.
