Brian James is a senior weather producer and Saturday evening meteorologist for KXAS-TV NBC 5. He is a Broadcast Meteorologist with more than 25 years of experience in weather analysis and forecasting and a forensic and consulting meteorologist.

James has been a part of the American Meteorological Society from September 1995 till today. He has spent the majority of his 20+ years as a degreed meteorologist in broadcast meteorology. He’s also a recipient of the American Meteorological Society’s (AMS) Seal of Approval.

Who Is Brian James Meteorologist?

Brian James is a meteorologist based in Dallas – Fort Worth. He earned a degree in bachelor of science in meteorology from the University of Oklahoma in 1997. James worked as a storm chaser for KOCO-TV in Oklahoma City throughout college, which led to his first career in television. After graduation, he went straight to work for KOCO-TV as a weather producer and storm chase coordinator.

Brian then stepped into the front of the camera when he obtained a job at WGKI-TV in Cadillac, Michigan. His next assignment took him to WEYI-TV in Flint, Michigan, where he worked as a morning meteorologist for three years.

Brian moved to Texas in 2003 and worked for KVII-TV in Amarillo from 2003 until 2007. From 2008 to 2015, he returned to KVII for a second time.

Brian has become a radar specialist for NBCUniversal in addition to his on-air duties on NBC5. He spends time visiting other NBC and Telemundo stations, training and assisting them in the optimal use of their radars.
