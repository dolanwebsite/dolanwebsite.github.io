Kevin Lemanowicz Bay News 9

Lemanowicz is a well-known weather forecaster. Currently, he serves as a broadcast & digital meteorologist at Bay News 9. Moreover, he has done forecasts to the Boston Red Sox, Country 102.5 WKLB, Magic 106.7 WMJX, and WCRN AM 830.

Prior, Kevin worked as an on-camera meteorologist at WeatherNation. Likewise, he has worked for WBNG TV 12 as a broadcast meteorologist and MMJ. It is known that he was the first chief meteorologist for Boston 25 News at Ten in 1996.

Most noteworthy, Lemanowicz is a Certified Broadcast Meteorologist (CBM) and is a Silver Circle Honoree. In addition, he is a special recognition from the Boston/New England chapter of the National Academy of Television Arts & Sciences.

Citing his career, Kevin has done report coverage of the President’s Day Storm of 2004. He has also covered the Blizzard of 2005 and the record snowfall in the winter of 2015. He was on air when a tornado hit Monson and other towns in 2011.

Kevin Lemanowicz WTIC

Lemanowicz worked as a chief meteorologist at WTIC in Hartford, CT. Prior, he had worked at the New England Weather Service in Hartford. While here, he did forecasts for private industry and radio stations.

Likewise, Kevin is part of the Massachusetts Middle School Scholars, Weston Observatory Lecture Series, and at the annual STEM Day at Fenway Park. Furthermore, he serves as the emcee for the annual Cox Conserves Heroes Awards.

Most noteworthy, Lemanowicz was a staff meteorologist at Fleetweather, Inc. Moreover, he tracked storms for radio, schools, private industry, and the Weather Channel. It is known that he started his career as a meteorologist at WVIT-TV in Hartford, CT.

On the other hand, Kevin is a member of the Juvenile Diabetes Research Foundation. He is also a strong supporter of The Random Smile Project, and The Salvation Army of MA. Before this, he interned under the former chief meteorologist, Pete Bouchard for WHDH-TV.
