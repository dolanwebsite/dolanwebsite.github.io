Kelly LeBrock Parents: Meet Mary Cecilia Traynor, Harold Arthur Le Brock

 Posted on November 2, 2023  |  1 minutes  |  189 words  |  Lynna Burgamy

Kelly LeBrock is a retired American model and actress. She is the daughter of Mary Cecilia Traynor and Harold Arthur Le Brock.

Kelly LeBrock was born in New York City and raised in London’s affluent Kensington district. Her father is Canadian-French, and her mother, Maria, is British.

Kelly LeBrock initially gained prominence as a model, appearing in various magazines and advertisements. She became widely known for her appearance on the cover of the iconic 1985 film “Weird Science.”

Kelly LeBrock’s breakthrough role came in the film “The Woman in Red” (1984), in which she starred alongside Gene Wilder. She gained further recognition for her role as Lisa, the computer-generated fantasy woman, in the aforementioned film “Weird Science” (1985), directed by John Hughes.

Throughout the 1980s and early 1990s, Kelly LeBrock appeared in several other movies, including “Hard to Kill” (1990), “Wrongfully Accused” (1998), and “Zoophilia” (2005). However, she gradually transitioned away from acting and focused more on her personal life.

In recent years, Kelly LeBrock has kept a relatively low public profile. She has stepped away from the entertainment industry and has primarily focused on her family and personal interests.
