Jay Freeman (also known as “Saurik”) is an American businessman and software developer who lives in the city of New York City. He is well recognised for his work on the Cydia application suite, which he founded. Professor Freeman studied Computer Science and graduated from the College of Creative Studies at the University of California at Santa Barbara before continuing his studies and earning a doctorate in computer science from UC Berkeley. The iPhone Dev Team development community counts him among its members (for the iPhone OS). 

For Apple’s iOS operating system, he built the Cydia software programme, which enables the phone to be unlocked so that it may run apps produced by third parties. In honour of the codling moth, Cydia pomonella, whose frequent habit of burrowing into apples inspired his moniker, he was given the name “Pomonella.” On September 10, 2010, Freeman announced that his company, Saurik IT LLC, had acquired Rock Your Phone, Inc. (best known for their Rock App) in order to make Cydia the largest application suite for the iOS platform, allowing users to jailbreak, or unlock, the platform to allow third-party applications to be installed.
