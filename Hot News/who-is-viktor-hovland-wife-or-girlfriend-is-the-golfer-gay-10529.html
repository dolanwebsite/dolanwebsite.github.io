Viktor Hovland Early Life

Victor Hovland started playing golf when he was 11 years old, when his father, who worked in America, sent him equipment. His father taught him the game, and he went to an indoor driving range on a daily basis to practice.

He was a member of the golf team at Oklahoma State University. He was the Norwegian Amateur Golf Championship winner in 2014.

By winning the 2018 U.S. Amateur, Victor became the first Norwegian player to do so. He was then invited to the 2019 Masters Tournament, 2019 U.S. Open, and 2019 Open Championship.

Following the 2019 U.S. Open, Hovland turned professional and made his professional debut in the 2019 Travelers Championship. He forfeited his automatic entrance for the 2019 Open Championship by doing so.

Similarly, when Hovland won the Puerto Rico Open in February 2020, he became the first Norwegian to win on the PGA Tour. By birdieing the 72ndhole at the Mayakoba Golf Classic in December 2020, he earned his second PGA Tour victory and first at a full-strength PGA Tour event.

In June 2021, Hovland became the first Norwegian to win on the European Tour when he won the BMW International Open.
