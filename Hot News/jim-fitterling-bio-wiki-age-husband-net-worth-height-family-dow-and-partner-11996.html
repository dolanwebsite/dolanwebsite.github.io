    Jim Fitterling Dow

    Junior Positions

    Fitterling was hired by The Dow Chemical Company in 1984. He became CEO of Filmtec Corporation, a subsidiary of Dow in 1998. Still, in 1998, Fitterling became the global business director of Dow’s liquid separations unit. He was named both general manager of Dow Thailand and managing director of the SCC – Dow Group of joint venture companies in 2000.

    Fitterling became CEO of The OPTIMAL Group in 2002, an affiliate company of both Dow and Petroliam Nasional Berhad. He began overseeing OPTIMAL’s regions in Southeast Asia and Australia in 2004.

    In 2005, Fitterling left OPTIMAL Group to become the business vice president of polyethylene at Dow Chemical Corporation. From 2007 until 2009, he then served as Dow’s president of basic plastics. Soon Fitterling was also a member of several committees at Dow, including the Executive Leadership Committee, the Management Committee, and the Strategy Board.

    Jim Fitterling was given in 2012, “executive oversight of feedstocks, performance plastics in Asia and Latin America.” While still an executive vice president, in November 2012 Fitterling was appointed a member of Dow’s newly formed executive committee.

    Senior Positions

    Fitterling became vice chairman of business operations for Dow in 2014. He was named chief operating officer of Dow in October 2015, which announced its merger with DuPont in December 2015.

    Fitterling then worked with Dow’s CEO on reforming the new company into three separate entities, and to later be COO of the new Dow company. He was ranked 73 on the Top 100 LGBT Leaders list published by The Financial Times in 2015, ranking 28 the following year.

    Dow Chemical announced on February 2, 2016, that Fitterling would be replacing Andrew Liveris as Dow president upon Liveris’ retirement in 2017. Fitterling was named Dow’s president and chief operating officer in 2016. He also served as COO of the materials science division of DowDuPont from September 2017 to March 2019.

    As of 2017, he was still Dow’s president and COO. Fitterling was named on March 12, 2018, as the CEO of the new Dow Inc., which was split, on April 1, 2019, from DowDuPont, as the new parent company of The Dow Chemical Company.

    Jim Fitterling Appearances and Awards

    Jim Fitterling has appeared in the media as a Dow representative. At the 2015 Manufacturing Leadership Summit, he was the keynote speaker and has also spoken at the IHS World Petrochemical Conference in Houston, Texas.

    In celebration of National Coming Out Day, in 2014 Fitterling came out to Dow’s employees following a bout with cancer. He is an advocate for diversity in corporate and educational settings and, in 2016, was quoted by The Wall Street Journal on Dow’s public campaign against anti-gay rights legislation in states such as North Carolina, Mississippi, Tennessee, and Georgia.

    Fitterling was named #1 of the “Top 100 LGBT+ Executives” on The Financial Times’ annual “OUTstanding in Business” list in 2018. He was featured in the Bloomberg Businessweek article “How Dow Chemical Got Woke” in March 2019.
