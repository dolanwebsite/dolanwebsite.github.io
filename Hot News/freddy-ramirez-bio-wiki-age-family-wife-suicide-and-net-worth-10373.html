Freddy Ramirez Career

Since 1995, Freddy Ramirez has dedicated himself to the Miami-Dade Police Department. He first had his professional beginning with the designation of Police Officer Trainee from 1995 until 1996. Then, he served four years as an uninformed patrol officer, two years in Detective-Agriculture Patrol Unit, and one year in Sergeant- Uniform Patrol.

He also served as Sergeant- Narcotics Bureau, Lieutenant-Hammocks District-Uniform Patrol for one year, then Lieutenant in the general investigation unit for two years, became a captain in the Narcotics Bureau for three years, major in the Homicide Bureau for one year, and then became the chief of Criminal Investigation Division for another one year.

Freddy gave 19 years to the police department, having different designations and promotions. After that, he was promoted to assistant director of investigative services for one year, assistant director of police services for two years, deputy director for one year, and then director for another two years.

Beginning as assistant director, he gave nearly additional seven years to the police department. Since March 2022, he has been working as Interim Chief of Public Safety based in Miami-Dade County. Freddy gave 26 years to the law enforcement profession.

Besides having a fully-fledged career in the police department, Freddy Ramirez is also a member of various associations like the International Association of Chiefs of Police, Major County Sheriffs Association, Police Executive Research Forum, Florida Police Chiefs Association, Eleventh Judicial Circuit Advisory Board, Dade-Miami Criminal Justice Council, and Miami-Dade County Association of Chiefs of Police. Also, he is the president of the Police Officer Assistant Trust.
