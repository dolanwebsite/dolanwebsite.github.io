    Ron Lea Actor

    Ron Lea landed his first feature film in the year 1981. This was when he made an appearance in the horror movie, ‘Happy Birthday to Me.’ Additionally, he has also appeared in a number of films and television series. They include; ‘The Blue Man’, ‘The Phone Call’, ‘Red Earth, among others.

    He rose to fame in the year 1990. This was after he got the character of Brian Malony in ‘Street Legal’. He remained a part of the show for four years. For his role, he was nominated for, the Gemini Award for Best Performance in a Supporting Role.

    Continuing his work in the acting field, he received the chance to work in ‘Doc’. He directed a few episodes while playing in ‘Doc as Dr. Oliver Crane. In 2008, he played in a television movie, ‘Victor’,. For his role, he received his second Gemini Awards nomination.

    In 2013, he bagged a role for the character Lieutenant Gavin Hardcastle, in the series ‘Orphan Black’. The following year, he worked for six episodes in the series ‘The Best Laid Plans. That same year, he appeared in numerous productions with various companies.

    He was a part of,‘Covert Affairs’, ‘Mr. Cartwright, ‘Dr. Cabbie’, ‘Touring T. O’, ‘ Brick Mansions’, and ‘ Reign’. Most recently, in 2017, he appeared in a television movie ‘Framed for Murder: A Fixer Upper Mystery’, as Pete Hughes.
