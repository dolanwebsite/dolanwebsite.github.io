What Was Mercedes ISD Superintendent Carolyn Mendiola Arrested For?

 Posted on November 15, 2023  |  3 minutes  |  453 words  |  Lynna Burgamy

On Friday, Mercedes ISD Superintendent Carolyn Mendiola was arrested after a months-long scandal in the district.

Carolyn Ann Mendiola was born and reared in the Rio Grande Valley, and she has spent her whole career as a teacher in South Texas. She graduated from PSJA High School and went on to the University of Texas-Pan American for her Master’s and bachelor’s degrees.

Similarly, she graduated from the University of Texas at Arlington with a Superintendent Certification in 2013. She commenced her teaching career as a high school English Language Arts teacher at La Joya ISD in 1994.

During her ten years in La Joya ISD, she held a variety of administrative positions, including Secondary Language Arts Coordinator for the district.

Recently, a deluge of news about her arrest has just surfaced on the internet. Here’s what we’ve learned thus far.

What Was Mercedes ISD Superintendent Carolyn Mendiola Arrested For?

According to sources, Mercedes ISD Superintendent Carolyn Mendiola was arrested on Friday as a result of months of controversies in the district.

Officer Juan Morales verified that Mendiola was scheduled to be prosecuted in Alamo Municipal Court just after 2 p.m., and Mercedes school board President Oscar Hernandez said the school district’s counsel informed him of a warrant for Mendiola’s arrest shortly before noon.

According to Hernandez, the warrant is relevant to a current investigation. Several district workers have lately been arrested for a range of crimes.

It was not immediately apparent what charges Mendiola is facing or why she is being held in Alamo.

Carolyn Mendiola Charges and Scandal Detail

As previously stated, there is no visible information concerning Carolyn Mendiola’s charges and scandals on the internet.

Daisy Espinoza, a district spokesman, said she was unaware of the arrest. When asked about the arrest, Mercedes Interim Police Chief Blanca Sanchez indicated she will issue a statement.

Moreover, according to some reports, Mendiola stated her decision to leave the school system early in the 2022-2023 academic year.

Some Facts About Carolyn Mendiola

In 2004, Mendiola moved to Mission, Texas, where she worked for 8 years as an Assistant Principal in Sharyland ISD until taking a job as a Middle School Administrator.

During her time in that position, she began investigating and launching Sharyland ISD’s Early College High School Model.

Likewise, she is recognized for founding the Sharyland Advanced Academic Academy, the first Early College High School, in 2014 and serving as its first campus principal.

Mendiola was appointed as Sharyland High School Principal shortly after, and she and her team worked hard for three years to win 21 out of 21 distinctions in the Texas Accountability System.
