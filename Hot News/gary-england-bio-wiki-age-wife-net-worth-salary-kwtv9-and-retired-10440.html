    Gary England Channel 9 (KWTV)

    England is a vastly recognized former chief meteorologist. He worked at KWTV (Channel 9) and was the first on-air meteorologist to give an alert of a possible tornado using a commercial Doppler weather radar. He is known for contributing to the invention of the First Warning map graphic.

    Currently, Gary serves as the Vice President of Corporate Relations and Weather Development at Griffin Communications LLC. Citing his work, he is credited with issuing the first televised Doppler weather radar bulletin for a tornado, in March 1982.

    It is known that England began to work at KWTV on October 16, 1972. Afterward, he helped create the First Warning in 1990. He further helped create Storm Tracker in 1991, a computer program that provided the arrival time of severe weather for the audience.

    However, Gary confirmed that he was leaving KWTV in 2013. Afterward, he became the Vice President of Corporate Relations and Weather Development for Griffin Communications. He served as one of three’ weather announcers’ in the 1996 Twister film.

    Gary England Twister

    England served as one of three’ weather announcers’ in the 1996 Twister film. It is an American epic disaster action film with a screenplay by Michael Crichton and Anne-Marie Martin. The film directed by Jan de Bont in 1996.

    On the other hand, Steven Spielberg, Walter Parkes, Laurie MacDonald, and Gerald R. Molen were executive producers. The movie stars Helen Hunt, Bill Paxton, Jami Gertz, and Cary Elwes, depicting a group of storm chasers investigating tornadoes during a serious Oklahoma outbreak.
