Who are Connie and Larry on tattletales?

Larry Coates and Conny Van Dyke were a prominent couple who graced the television screens on the popular TV show “Tattletales” in 1975. Larry Coates, an Ohio disc jockey, and Conny Van Dyke, were married for six years, from 1971 to 1976.

“Tattletales” was an American game show produced by Goodson-Todman Productions in association with Fremantle. The show had two runs on the CBS daytime schedule between February 1974 and June 1984 and was hosted by Bert Convy.

The premise of the show involved questions about celebrity couples’ personal lives and was based on “He Said, She Said,” a syndicated Goodson-Todman show that aired during the 1969-1970 season. The show was rebooted in 2021 as “About Last Night,” featuring celebrity couples playing for charity and physical challenges.

The show’s format involved celebrities and their spouses playing for sections of the studio audience, trying to match answers to questions about their personal lives.

The show had several announcers, including Jack Clark, Gene Wood, Johnny Olson, and John Harlan, who provided the voiceover at various times. “Tattletales” was a beloved game show that entertained audiences with its unique premise and celebrity participants.

The reboot in 2021 as “About Last Night” shows the enduring appeal of the concept, with celebrity couples playing for charity and engaging in physical challenges.
