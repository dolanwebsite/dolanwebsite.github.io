Julia Hartley-Brewer Biography

Julia Hartley-Brewer is an English Radio Presenter, Political Journalist, and Newspaper Columnist, who was born and brought up in Birmingham, England. Currently, she works at talkRADIO in London, England, as the presenter of the weekday breakfast radio show. She presents the weekday radio show from 10:00 am to 1:00 pm from 21 March 2016 to 12 January 2018, and from 6:30 am to 10:00 am since 15 January. Previously, before joining talkRADIO, she worked as an LBC presenter for three years, from February 2011 to December 2014.

    Julia Hartley-Brewer Talk Radio

    Hartley had her debut in journalism at the East London Advertiser in Bethnal Green. She later got to be employed as a news reporter and political correspondent for the London Evening Standard. She later joined The Guardian, where she worked until September 2000. She then relocated to the Sunday Express as a political correspondent, then political editor from 2001 until 2007 and then assistant editor (Politics), with a byline on a weekly opinion column. She later left the Sunday Express in February 2011.

    She had presented and narrated two political documentaries for the television channels BBC Two and BBC Four about the history of British Deputy Prime Ministers, called Every Prime Minister Needs a Willie, and also the history of the Leader of the Opposition in The Worst Job in Politics in 2006. She has also been one of the panelists on the comedy quiz show Have I Got News for You seven times as well as being a regular panelist on BBC One’s Question Time and Radio 4’s Any Questions.

    She is as well a regular pundit and commentator on TV and radio, including for Sky News, the BBC News Channel, BBC One’s The One Show, ITV’s Tonight show, Lorraine on ITV, This Morning on ITV, The Agenda on ITV, Sunday Politics on BBC1, BBC Radio 5 Live and BBC Radio 4’s Today and PM programs. She as well appeared as a contestant on Pointless Celebrities in October 2014, winning the prize for her chosen charity, the Miscarriage Association.

    She was also s an LBC presenter from February 2011to December 2014  and was replaced by Shelagh Fogarty. She currently broadcasts on Talkradio, a talk radio station that was launched on 21 March 2016.   Talkradio announced that she would be moving from the mid-morning slot to the 6.30 am breakfast show back in 2018. Her show The Julia Hartley-Brewer Show was launched on YouTube; under the Talkradio brand, each program is a one-to-one interview with a guest.

    She supported Britain leaving the European Union during the EU referendum of that year in 2016. Her allegations against the then Defence Secretary Sir Michael Fallon in 2017. She recounted an incident 15 years earlier when he repeatedly touched her knee throughout dinner in 2002, may have contributed to his eventual resignation.

    She spoke at the Leave Means Leave rally in Parliament Square, London. She was invited by The Royal College of General Practitioners to speak in an ‘NHS Question Time’ panel debate at their annual conference in 2019 but withdrew the invitation after 700 GPs signed a petition complaining that her views were not conducive to the work they were doing to promote inclusivity within the profession and amongst patients.
