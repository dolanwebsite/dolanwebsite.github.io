Obituary Notice Of Brendon Grimshaw From Seychelles?

Seychelles Weekly released an obituary notice for Brendon Grimshaw in 2012. According to them, he passed away after suffering from some illness. However, it is not confirmed if his cause of death was an illness.

People love and appreciate Grimshaw’s story of how he owned Moyenne Island and turned it into his home. Then, in 1962, he went on vacation to Seychelles. It was an archipelago of 115 islands in the Indian Ocean with only eight regularly inhabited islands.

He was an editor, working for several of East Africa’s largest publications. In addition, at the time, Tanzania had gained independence the previous year, and Kenya would follow a year later.

Thus, Grimshaw knew that jobs like his would soon be handed up to locals. As a result, he sought a new route in life, one that brought him closer to nature, knowing that he would soon be unemployed.

He dreamt about having property in Seychelles, ideally his island.

Find Brendon Grimshaw Story On Wikipedia

The Wikipedia profile of Moyenne Island explains the story of its owner, Brendon Grimshaw. On the second-to-last day of his visit, Rene Antoine Lafortune approached him and asked him if he wanted to buy an island.

Finally, he bought the island for £8,000 and made it livable. He set up his island with the help of Rene. Visitors were charged €12 to come ashore, wander the island, dine at the “Jolly Roger” restaurant, and relax on the beach.

Grimshaw and his friend planted 16,000 trees and erected 4.8 kilometers (3.0 miles) of nature walks. He also brought and reared Aldabra giant tortoises to create an island of breathtaking beauty.

Hence, the island is home to roughly 120 gigantic tortoises and diverse plant and birdlife.
