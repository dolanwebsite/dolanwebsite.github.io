Brian Baldinger Biography

Brian Baldinger (born Brian David Baldinger) is a former professional American football offensive lineman in the National Football League for the Dallas Cowboys, Indianapolis Colts, and Philadelphia Eagles. He covered Philadelphia Eagles preseason games as an analyst with Scott Graham for several years. He currently works for NFL Network, where he serves as an analyst for the television show NFL Total Access.

Brian Baldinger Career and Education

He initially enrolled at the Naval Academy after high school, but later transferred to Nassau Community College, where he became an All-Coastal Conference tight end and also practiced basketball. He transferred to Duke University in 1979, where he was converted to a guard due to his blocking ability. He was named the team’s most improved player as a senior and started all 11 games.

Professional career

Dallas Cowboys

Baldinger was signed as an undrafted free agent by the Dallas Cowboys on April 30, 1982, following the 1982 NFL Draft. As a rookie, he appeared in four of nine games, mostly on special teams. In 1983, he saw action as a backup center and guard.

In 1984, he started two games at right tackle to replace an injured Phil Pozderac and two games at right guard to replace an injured Kurt Petersen. In the third preseason game against the Chicago Bears in 1985, he injured his right knee and was placed on the injured reserve list.

In 1986, he was a backup center who also filled in as a third tight end in short-yardage situations. He was placed on the injured reserve list on September 2, 1987, due to a left knee injury suffered in a preseason game.

He was added to the regular-season roster on October 24. In six of the final eight games, he was declared inactive. Baldinger was not re-signed at the end of the season. He played every offensive line position and a few snaps at tight end during his time with the Cowboys.

Indianapolis Colts

On July 19, 1988, he was signed as a free agent by the Indianapolis Colts to provide depth in the event of guard Ron Solt’s lengthy contract holdout.  He was the offensive line’s lightest member.

He appeared in 16 games and started three of them at right tackle. Against the Green Bay Packers, he caught his first career pass (37 yards) from a tackle-eligible position. He played in all 16 games in 1989.

He started the season opener at right tackle in place of Kevin Call and two games at left tackle in place of Chris Hinton, who was injured. In short-yardage situations, he was also used as a tackle-eligible player.

For the first eight games of the 1990 season, he was named the starting right guard. He was moved to right tackle Call’s spot in the second half of the season after he injured his left shoulder in the eighth game against the New York Giants.

In 1991, he started 13 games at center in place of Ray Donaldson, who was sidelined for the season after breaking his leg against the Los Angeles Raiders.

Buffalo Bills

On April 2, 1992, he was signed in Plan B free agency by the Buffalo Bills, joining his brother Gary Baldinger. He was released on August 31.

Philadelphia Eagles

The Philadelphia Eagles signed him as a free agent on September 28, 1992, to replace an injured John Hudson. He started four games at right guard in 1993, filling in for an injured Eric Floyd. On June 4, 1994, he and 11 other veterans were released as part of a salary-cap move.

Broadcasting career

Baldinger began his broadcasting career in 1995 as a color analyst at Bucknell University before moving to Fox in 1997 to provide analysis for NFL Europe games.

His soothing voice and handy repertoire of clichés impressed the network, and he was promoted to a color commentary slot for NFL games. Baldinger collaborated with play-by-play announcers Ray Bentley, Curt Menefee, Joe Buck, Pat Summerall, Kenny Albert, and Dick Stockton.

In May 2009, it was reported that Baldinger would be replaced on Fox telecasts by former NFL safety John Lynch. Baldinger was soon hired as the lead analyst for Compass Media Networks’ national radio broadcasts of select Sunday afternoon NFL games.

During football season, Baldinger also co-hosts a talk show on Sporting News Radio and teaches seminars for Nadia Communications. He is the author of The Map to Clear Messages.

Baldinger has previously co-hosted several radio shows and is now a regular contributor to a sports-talk radio show on Philadelphia’s “97.5 The Fanatic,” an ESPN affiliate.
