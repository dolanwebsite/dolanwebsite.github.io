Tennis Player Naomi Osaka Weight Gain Reason

During the 2018 US Open, she was suffering from depression which was one of the main reasons for gaining weight. 

The news came out as she refused to participate in required press conferences during the French Open and was subsequently fined $15,000 in May of 2021. 

She later withdrew from the event to deal with her mental health and well-being on 31 May 2021. She again pulled out of Wimbledon, citing that she was taking some personal time with her family and friends less than a month later. 

Due to depression, she lost to Leylah Fernandez, and during the process, she threw her racket thrice and received a code violation for firing a ball at the spectators.  

Osaka was to the point of tears, after which she had to seek therapy at the 2022 Indian Wells Open. She said that the treatment helped her improve her health. She is now prepared for the future to tackle such an incident again. 

She has also talked about her ups and downs during an interview. 

Know about Naomi Osaka Family Ethnicity and Life

Naomi was born on 16 October 1997. She was born in Osaka, Japan. She was born in the family of Tamaki Osaka and Leonard Francois. She was raised by her parents and her older sister “Mari Osaka.”

Naomi’s sister is also a former professional tennis player. Their parents met when her father was visiting Hokkaido while a college student in New York. Osaka is of Japanese and Haitian Ethnicity. She also has dual citizenship in Japan and America. 

She moved to the United States at the age of 3, completing her basic training. She attended her education at the Elmont Alden Terrace Primary School, and after graduation, she enrolled at Broward Virtual Middle High school. 

Later, she attended the Florida Tennis SBT Academy and the ProWorld Tennis Academy. 

Naomi Osaka has a Massive Net Worth

The professional and talented tennis player “Naomi Osaka” has a massive net worth in her life. It has been estimated that she has a net worth of $45 million. Talking about her salary, she earned $38 Million in 2020. Just a year later, she made nearly $60 Million in 2021. 

She was also the highest-paid female athlete in history. She earned more than the previous long-standing highest-paid female athlete, Serena Williams, by $5 Million. Her hard work and dedication led her to earn such huge net worth and salary in her life.
