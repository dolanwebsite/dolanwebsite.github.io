Max Armstrong Biography

Max Armstrong is a renowned American Agriculture Broadcaster. Currently, he is working as a Director of Broadcasting at Farm Progress since July 2009. He works as the Producer and Host of the ‘Farm Progress America’ and ‘Max Armstrong’s Midwest Digest’ programs that are aired daily on radio.

The two programs are aired on over 140 local radio stations including WRAY, WITY, KMAQ, KFEQ among others. He also works as a Co-Host & Co-Founder for This Week In Agribusiness in Batavia Illinois since Aug 2005 and has been working there for over 15 years.

He also serves as the Voice-Over Announcer, Meeting Program Moderator at Super M. Inc in Chicago, Illinois since November 1977, where his voice was heard and recognised. In 2017, he was the President of National Association of Farm Broadcasting in Platte City, Missouri.

    Max Armstrong Broadcast

    His first job out of college was as a Broadcast Editor for the Illinois Farm Bureau where he met Orion Samuelson. The two have partnered together 1st on WGN radio, and later on the TV Show U.S.  Farm Report, until the show This Week in Agribusiness.

    Armstrong was the director of broadcasting for Farm Progress. Also, he produces and hosts the Farm Progress America and Max Armstrong’s Midwest Digest daily radio programs. He also hosts a three-minute feature, “Farming America”, on the Tribune Radio Network.

    From 1977 to 2009, Armstrong was the agri-business broadcaster for WGN Radio before the program transitioned to a weekly Saturday morning show and accompanying podcast entitled The Morning Show with Orion and Max.

     This Week in Agribusiness

    Since August 2005, Armstrong has been co-host and co-founder of This Week in AgriBusiness, owned and produced with Samuelson. The show is a weekly agriculture television show, produced by OMAX Communications, LLC in cooperation with 22 Creative Group and broadcast on RFD-TV

    Max Armstrong National Association of Farm Broadcasting (NAFB)

    In 2001, Armstrong was named Farm Broadcaster of the Year by the NAFB.  However, In 2017, he was elected President of the NAFB. He was a member of NAFB for 40 years before being elected president.

    Max Armstrong WGN Radio Walk of Fame

    In 2016, Armstrong earned a plaque on the WGN Radio Walk of Fame in Chicago. He was full-time with WGN Radio for 32 years until 2009. The bronze plaque is in the sidewalk outside the Tribune Tower studios at 435 North Michigan Avenue in Chicago. However, the induction ceremony took place on May 27, 2016, where he was honored along with 6 others.

    Max Armstrong Tractor Shed

    Armstrong has a segment called “Max’s Tractor Shed,” on  This Week in Agribusiness, which features vintage tractors sent in by viewers

    Max Armstrong Philanthropy

    Armstrong contributed his time as an appointed Fire Commissioner in the Western Suburbs of Chicago for 22 years. Serving on a three-member board, he hires and promotes full-time firefighters and paramedics for one of the highest-rated (ISO 1) fire service organizations in the United States, the Lisle-Woodridge Fire Protection District.

    Max Armstrong Awards and honors

    At the University he received honors as an Old Master in 2005 and he was recognized as a ”Noted Alumnus” in 2009. Also, he was awarded an Alum Certificate of Distinction from the College of Agriculture and a Sagamore of the Wabash, given by the Governor of Indiana.

    He was named an Honorary Master Farmer in 2018 by Prairie Farmer Magazine. Armstrong’s Tractor App received a Best of NAMA award, along with awards from the American Agricultural Editors’ Association and American Web Design Awards.
