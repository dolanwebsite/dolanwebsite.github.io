Susan Shapiro Biography

Susan Shapiro is an American news television journalist who was born in Philadelphia in the USA. She is the sole anchor of News 8 at Noon and is also the health reporter on the News 8 On Your Side Team. Shapiro started at WGAL in 1981 as a reporter and at various times also anchored the morning and weekend news reports.

Shapiro’s day begins with a 3:30 a.m. wake-up call and reaches WGAL’s Lancaster studio by 4:30. She co-anchors the NBC station’s 5 to 7 a.m. newscast and news updates during the Today show. When her on-air duties end, Shapiro covers breaking stories as well as works on in-depth reports for the 8 On Your Side Investigative Team.

    Susan Shapiro Career

    Before joining the News 8 staff, Shapiro was an anchor as well as a reporter at WTAJ-TV in Altoona, Pa. She has also worked as a research assistant for Philadelphia Magazine.

    Shapiro has received various awards and honors for her reporting which such as a 1992 Emmy Award for spot news, a 2005 Mid-Atlantic Region Emmy for individual achievement for feature reporting, a number of Associated Press Broadcasters Awards as well as the Pennsylvania Association of Broadcasters Award for her investigative report on puppy mills.

    Shapiro is among the Board of Governors of the Philadelphia Regional Chapter of the National Academy of Television Arts and Sciences, a past member of the board of Lancaster County Safe Kids as well as a current member of the board of the Rocky Springs Carousel Association.
