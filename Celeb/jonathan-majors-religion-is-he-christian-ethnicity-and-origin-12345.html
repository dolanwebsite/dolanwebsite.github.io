Jonathan Majors Religion: Is He Christian?

Yes, Jonathan Majors belong to a African-American Christian family.

Nevertheless, the actor has also read the Koran and the Torah and to appreciate Hindu and Buddhist teachings.

According to him, Christianity is still his primary guide and belief system. He refers to it as “my template, my first lexicon.” His mother, a pastor, was responsible for teaching him these values. 

Jonathan Majors attended Duncanville High School in Texas before studying acting at the University of North Carolina School of the Arts and the Yale School of Drama.

While at UNC, he also played football as a wide receiver before eventually focusing solely on acting.

Jonathan Majors: Ethnicity, Origin And Family

Jonathan Majors is an African American actor born in Texas, United States and grew up with his mother and father.

While his ancestry is primarily African American, he also mentioned Native American and European ancestry in interviews.

Jonathan’s mother is a pastor, and his father is a former Texas Ranger and a Vietnam War veteran.

Reportedly, Jonathan’s father left the family when he was five. The actor has two siblings, younger brother named Cameron and an older sister named Monica.

In interviews, Majors has mentioned that his father’s military background heavily influenced his childhood and perspective on the world.

Majors has also spoken about his family’s history and connection to the Civil Rights Movement.

Furthermore, he has credited his grandfather’s legacy as a significant influence on his activism and commitment to social justice causes.

His grandfather, Milton P. Curry, was a civil rights activist who worked alongside Martin Luther King Jr. and John Lewis.

Acting Career And Upcoming Projects Of Jonathan Majors

Majors began his acting career in 2017 with a role in the film “Hostiles”and gained wider recognition for his performance in the HBO series “Lovecraft Country” (2020).

The American actor played Atticus Freeman, a Black veteran who embarks on a road trip across 1950s America in search of his missing father.

For his performance in the series, Majors received a nomination for the Critics’ Choice Television Award for Best Actor in a Drama Series.

In 2021, Majors starred in the Netflix western “The Harder They Fall,” which received the NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Motion Picture.

He is widely known for playing the villain Kang the Conqueror in the recently released Marvel Cinematic Universe film “Ant-Man and the Wasp: Quantumania.”

One of Jonathan’s most anticipated upcoming movies is Avengers: The Kang Dynasty, scheduled to be released in 2025.
