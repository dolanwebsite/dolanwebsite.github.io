He Was Convicted Of Murder

Three men including Raphael Rowe were charged and arrested for the murder of Peter Hurburgh and violent robberies along the M25. 

Among the 3 men accused, Rowe had been identified by the other two and was put in jail. He always said that he was innocent. 

Rowe’s Conviction Was Deemed Wrongful, 12 years Later

After the then-19-year-old was convicted and put in jail in 1988 for the murder and robberies, he and the other 2 men accused of the crimes appeared in court. 

Their appeal was heard and 12 years later the Court of Appeals overturned their sentence saying that their conviction was wrong. 

He was originally sentenced to life, but served from 1988 to 2000. 

The Police Allegedly Conspired With Prosecutors

According to the journalist, he was rescued by the BBC Rough Justice program who investigated into his case and found evidence that Surrey police had conspired with the prosecutors.

This evidence was eventually used in the case of his appeal. In 1998 at the time of his arrest, the cops had just bottled another case and had to let the Guildford Four.

The BBC even alleged that Norman Duncan was paid £10,000 to present false evidence for the prosecution.

He Frequently Got Into Legal Trouble Before His Arrest

Rowe said that he frequently was on the wrong side of the law, he was even expelled from school. 

At age 17, he was arrested for robbery and put in a Young Offenders Institute where he spent 10 months. After this, he tried to straighten out and learn journalism when he was arrested and subsequently put in prison for 12 years. 

Rowe Is A BBC Journalist And Motivational Speaker

After getting out of prison, he started working with the BBC as a journalist. He worked for BBC 5 Live and made a living as a journalist. 

He has gone all around the world, speaking about his experiences and advocating the reform of the prison system in the UK.
