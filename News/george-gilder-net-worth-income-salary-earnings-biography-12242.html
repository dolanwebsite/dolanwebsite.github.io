George F. Gilder (November 29, 1939) is a co-founder of the Discovery Institute, as well as an American investor, writer, economist, techno-utopian advocate, Republican Party activist, and Republican Party activist. During the early months of the Reagan administration, his worldwide bestseller Wealth and Poverty presented a practical and moral argument for supply-side economics and capitalism, making him President Reagan’s most cited living author. Knowledge and Power: The  Information Theory of Capitalism and How It is Revolutionizing Our World, released in 2013, reconstructed economics in terms of Alan Turing and Claude Shannon’s information theory. He is the father of four children from his marriage to Nini Gilder. Gilder made a name for himself as a critic of feminism and government welfare programmes in the 1970s, claiming that they undermined the “sexual constitution” that civilised and socialised males in fatherly and provider roles. Through numerous publications and his newsletter, the Gilder Technology Report, he became an ardent advocate of technology and the Internet in the 1990s. George Gilder Fund Management, LLC is his company’s chairman.
