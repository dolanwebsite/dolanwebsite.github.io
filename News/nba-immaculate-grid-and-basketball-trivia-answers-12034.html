Immaculate Grid Basketball Explained

Basketball Immaculate Grid is a guessing game that tests the knowledge of the NBA fans on a 3-by-3 grid with three categories each on the top and on the side.

The categories might be teams, achievements, movies, NBA Championships, coaches, and many more. The categories or criteria are specified and the player has to name the correct player for the given specifics.

Immaculate Grid NBA Rules

The rules of the grid trivia are very simple and easy to follow. You have nine chances to fill nine boxes. Correct and incorrect answers are considered one chance, so you might lose if you miss out on one.

  • The grid‚Äôs rows and columns must be satisfied by selecting a player.
  • The player must have played in the NBA at least once in his basketball career.
  • While choosing a player for a cell with a team and an award as the criteria, the selected player must have won it while he represented that team.
  • The same goes for the season stats or scoring records and averages.
  • A player can only be selected once in the game even if he fits in multiple criteria.
  • You can choose from either active on inactive players.
