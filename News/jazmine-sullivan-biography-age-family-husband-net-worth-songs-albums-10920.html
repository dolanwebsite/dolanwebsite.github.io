Jazmine Sullivan Biography

Jazmine Sullivan is an American singer-songwriter from Philadelphia, Pennsylvania known for her debut album Fearless

Sullivan signed on to Jive Records at age fifteen. She recorded an album that was never released and was ultimately dropped from the label. Sullivan gave vocals to Kindred the Family Soul ‘s song, “I Am,” as well as background vocals to the song “Party’s Over,” and title track to their 2003 debut Surrender to Love, and it was during this session that she first met rapper Missy Elliott, who would continue to produce the majority of her debut album, Fearless, as well as tracks on Love Me Back.

Sullivan wrote and produced with producers Cool & Dre entitled “Say I” for her third album, So Amazin’ to Dre’s then-girlfriend Christina Milian.[14] The song became the lead single, peaking at #13 on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs and #21 on the Hot 100.[15] Sullivan’s breakthrough hit, “Need U Bad,” was released in May 2008.

The song, which featured additional vocals by Salt-n-Pepa’s Missy Elliott and Sandy “Pepa” Denton, later made its debut on the Billboard Hot 100 at number 37 and spent a total of 34 weeks atop the Hot R&B / Hip Hop Songs.

Jazmine Sullivan Fearless

Her debut studio album Fearless was released through J Records on September 23, 2008. Production for the album took place during 2007 to 2008 and was handled by several record producers, including Missy Elliott, Stargate, Carvin & Ivan, Wyclef Jean, and Salaam Remi. The album debuted on the US Billboard 200 chart to number six, selling 65,000 copies in its first week. It also produced four singles on Billboard ‘s Hot 100 chart which achieved respectable charting. It Upon its publication, Fearless generally received favorable reviews from most music critics who complimented its production and the vocals of Sullivan, comparing it to female R&B artists like Alicia Keys
