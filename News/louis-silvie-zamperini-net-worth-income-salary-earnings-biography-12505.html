Louis Silvie Zamperini was an Olympic athlete and a Japanese prisoner during World War II. He was born on January 26, 1917, in Olean, New York State, USA, to Italian parents. The National Italian-American Sports Hall of Fame inducted Zamperini in 2008. His life was chronicled in Laura Hillenbrand’s book “Invincible,” which was turned into the Angelina Jolie-directed film “Invincible” (2014). In the same year, he died.
